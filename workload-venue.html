CoreCloud 3.1

The future is not cloud-first. It is workload-first.

Every workload has a best venue. CoreCloud helps organisations determine the optimal placement across public cloud, private cloud, hosted infrastructure, on-prem and hybrid models.

Venue Decision Factors

Placement should be driven by economics, risk and performance.

Cloud repatriation, AI infrastructure cost, sovereignty, latency and compliance are forcing organisations to rethink first-wave cloud assumptions.

Cost

Predictable workloads, large storage estates and data-heavy applications may not belong in public cloud by default.

Compliance

Regulated industries need architectures that respect governance, retention and audit requirements.

Sovereignty

Data locality and operational control increasingly matter for financial services, public sector and critical industries.

Performance

Latency, throughput and user experience define whether the venue is fit for purpose.

Scalability

Some workloads need elasticity. Others need stable infrastructure economics.

Business Value

The final decision should improve the business, not satisfy a vendor agenda.
